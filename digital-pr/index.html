Coverage

What strong coverage looks like.

Great digital PR should do more than create a short-lived spike. The best campaigns earn relevant placements that build brand presence and continue to support search performance long after the campaign closes.

01 — Relevance

Coverage in the right places

Not just any link or mention. The goal is placement in publications, industry titles, and topical environments that make genuine sense for the brand and the story being told.

02 — Authority

Signals that compound

High-quality placements influence perception well beyond the campaign window — supporting branded search, link profile strength, and the wider trust signals that matter to search engines.

03 — Narrative

A story people remember

The strongest campaigns are built around a clear angle a journalist can use quickly — original data, a sharp expert take, or a timely point of view tied to something already in the news.

04 — Measurement

Outcomes beyond mention counts

I look at placement quality, topical relevance, referral visibility, and how coverage feeds the broader search strategy — not just a raw number of mentions or a domain authority score.

Focus

Digital PR campaigns built with a clear connection to brand visibility and organic search performance — not just coverage for coverage's sake.
